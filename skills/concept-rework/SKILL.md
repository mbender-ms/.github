# Concept-Rework — Worldbuilding Redesign Skill

Redesign worldbuilding concepts that have been flagged as echoing specific published works. The goal is to preserve the concept's narrative function while creating a structure that is genuinely original.

## When to Use

- After a concept-originality-scan flags a concept as ECHO or LEAN
- When the author recognizes a structural parallel during worldbuilding
- During revision when beta readers or editors flag a concept as derivative

---

## Rework Process

### Step 1: Define What the Concept DOES for the Story

Before changing anything, articulate exactly what narrative work this concept performs:

- **Plot function**: What events does this concept enable? What conflicts does it create?
- **Character function**: How does it shape character motivations, relationships, and growth?
- **Thematic function**: What themes does it embody or explore?
- **World texture**: How does it make the world feel lived-in and specific?
- **Reader engagement**: What makes this concept interesting to a reader? What's the hook?

These functions are NON-NEGOTIABLE. Every alternative must fulfill all of them.

### Step 2: Identify What Makes It Derivative

Pinpoint the specific structural elements that create the echo:

- Is it the **hierarchy** (e.g., exactly 4 tiers, just like the source)?
- Is it the **mechanism** (e.g., a substance that grants abilities, just like spice)?
- Is it the **relationship between elements** (e.g., houses competing for a single throne)?
- Is it the **progression** (e.g., power awakens through emotional trauma, just like ACOTAR)?
- Is it the **aesthetic** (e.g., elemental magic mapped to personality types)?

### Step 3: Generate 3 Alternatives

Each alternative must:
1. Fulfill ALL narrative functions identified in Step 1
2. Change the structural elements identified in Step 2
3. Be internally consistent (no logical contradictions)
4. Generate its own interesting story possibilities that the original didn't have
5. Not echo a DIFFERENT published work (don't fix a Dune echo by creating a Red Rising echo)

### Step 4: Evaluate and Compare

| Criterion | Alt A | Alt B | Alt C |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fulfills plot function | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ |
| Fulfills character function | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ |
| Fulfills thematic function | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ |
| Originality (no new echoes) | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ |
| Internal consistency | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ | ✅/❌ |
| New story possibilities | [describe] | [describe] | [describe] |
| Downstream impact | [scope of changes needed] | [scope] | [scope] |
| Author appeal | [subjective assessment] | [assessment] | [assessment] |

### Step 5: Propagate the Selected Alternative

Update ALL documents that reference the redesigned concept:
- World bible
- Concept documents
- Character bible (if character motivations are affected)
- Political maps
- Beat sheets and outlines
- Series arc documents
- Any manuscript chapters already drafted

## Redesign Strategies

### Strategy: Inversion
Take the echoed concept and invert its core dynamic:
- Source has centralized power → your version has deliberately distributed power
- Source has hereditary abilities → your version has abilities that skip generations unpredictably
- Source has a single-resource economy → your version has competing resource dependencies

### Strategy: Merger
Combine two or more genre conventions into something new:
- Instead of "rival houses" (GoT) OR "academy factions" (ZA), create a system where faction allegiance is temporary and renegotiated each generation through a specific mechanism unique to your world

### Strategy: Technological Grounding
Take a concept that reads as "fantasy in space" and ground it in hard sci-fi technology:
- Instead of "elemental magic," derive abilities from specific quantum-neural interface interactions that have real (if extrapolated) physics
- Instead of "a prophesied chosen one," create a statistically inevitable convergence that was predicted by modeling, not prophecy

### Strategy: Consequence Extrapolation
Take a concept and ask "what would ACTUALLY happen if this existed for 10,000 years?"
- If rival factions existed for millennia, they wouldn't still be fighting the same way — they'd have evolved institutional countermeasures, marriage-based diplomacy, and information warfare
- If a bonding system existed, society would have built institutions around it — insurance, law, medicine, religion

### Strategy: Cultural Specificity
Make the concept feel like it grew from your world's specific history:
- Instead of a generic academy, design one that exists BECAUSE of a specific historical event in your timeline
- Instead of generic faction rivalry, create rivalries rooted in specific resource disputes, philosophical disagreements, or historical betrayals unique to your world

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Changing only the names/labels while keeping the structure identical — "Pillars instead of Houses" doesn't fix a structural echo
- ❌ Making the concept more generic to avoid similarity — generic is worse than derivative; be specific in a DIFFERENT direction
- ❌ Creating an alternative that only works for this one concept and contradicts the rest of the world — consistency matters
- ❌ Ignoring that the redesign might create a NEW echo with a different source
- ❌ Overcomplicating the concept to differentiate it — complexity for its own sake makes for bad fiction
- ❌ Redesigning a concept that was flagged as CONVENTION (acceptable) — don't fix what isn't broken
