# Scene-Rework — Narrative Beat Reimagination

Take a scene flagged by the originality checkers and reimagine it to achieve the same story function through a genuinely different execution.

## When to Use

- After a scene-echo-scan flags a specific scene as echoing a published work
- When the author recognizes a scene is "too close" during drafting
- During revision when beta readers note a similarity to a published work

---

## The Principle: Same Beat, Different Dance

Every scene in a novel serves a **narrative function** — what it accomplishes for character, plot, and theme. That function is not owned by anyone. But the specific **execution** — how the scene plays out moment by moment — can be too close to a published source.

The goal is to keep the function and completely reimagine the execution.

---

## Rework Process

### Step 1: Identify the Narrative Function
Answer these questions about the flagged scene:
- **What does this scene accomplish for the plot?** (e.g., reveals the antagonist's true allegiance)
- **What does it accomplish for character?** (e.g., forces the protagonist to choose between loyalty and survival)
- **What does it accomplish for theme?** (e.g., demonstrates that power corrupts even the well-intentioned)
- **What emotional response should the reader have?** (e.g., shock, devastation, grim satisfaction)
- **What state are the characters in AFTER this scene?** (e.g., alliance is broken, protagonist is alone)

These functions are preserved. Everything else is on the table.

### Step 2: Identify the Specific Echo
Answer these questions about the similarity:
- **What published scene does this echo?** (specific book, chapter, scene)
- **What elements are too similar?** (setting, choreography, dialogue pattern, twist mechanism, character positions)
- **Is the echo in the setup, execution, or aftermath?** (or all three?)
- **Would a reader of the source material notice?** (the casual reader, the dedicated fan, or only an obsessive analyst?)

### Step 3: Generate 3 Alternative Executions
For each alternative, change at LEAST these elements:
- **Setting/location**: Move the scene to a different physical space
- **Mechanism**: Change HOW the beat happens (a betrayal via poison becomes a betrayal via information leak)
- **POV positioning**: Change who sees what, who knows what, who acts first
- **Emotional sequence**: Change the order of emotions (shock-then-grief becomes creeping-dread-then-fury)
- **Aftermath**: Change the immediate consequences (instead of fleeing, the character stays and fights)

### Step 4: Evaluate Alternatives

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Originality** | High | Is this execution genuinely different from the flagged source? |
| **Narrative function** | High | Does it still accomplish everything the original scene needed to? |
| **Character consistency** | High | Do the characters behave in ways consistent with their established personalities? |
| **Emotional impact** | Medium | Is the new version as emotionally powerful as the original? (Or more?) |
| **Plot integration** | Medium | Does it fit smoothly with the scenes before and after? |
| **Thematic resonance** | Medium | Does it reinforce the same themes? |

## Common Scene Types That Need Reworking

### The Betrayal
- Don't: feast/wedding massacre (GoT), friend-turns-during-battle (generic)
- Do: Find a mechanism unique to your world's technology, politics, or bond system

### The Academy Trial
- Don't: arena combat with rankings (Empyrean/Red Rising), elemental testing (ZA)
- Do: Use your specific tech/communion system to create a trial that couldn't exist in another universe

### The First Kiss / Romantic Climax
- Don't: Follow the exact emotional sequence of a specific published scene
- Do: Ground it in THIS relationship's specific tensions, THIS world's constraints

### The Power Revelation
- Don't: Losing control during emotional crisis in a public setting (ACOTAR/ZA)
- Do: Find a trigger and manifestation unique to your power system

### The Political Coup
- Don't: Poison at a feast (GoT), assassination during ceremony (Dune)
- Do: Use your political structure's specific vulnerabilities

---

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Changing surface details but keeping the same emotional sequence beat-for-beat — readers will still recognize it
- ❌ Reworking a scene into something bland just to avoid the echo — the new version must be EQUALLY compelling
- ❌ Ignoring downstream effects — if you change HOW a betrayal happens, you may need to adjust the next 3 chapters
- ❌ Only changing the setting — moving a scene from a feast hall to a bridge doesn't make it original if the choreography is identical
- ❌ Losing the narrative function in the rework — the scene existed for a reason, and that reason must survive
