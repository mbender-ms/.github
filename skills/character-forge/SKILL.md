# Character-Forge — Character Creation Skill

Design complex, emotionally compelling characters and relationships for romantic hard sci-fi fiction.

## When to Use

- Creating a new character from scratch (protagonist, love interest, supporting cast)
- Designing romantic pairings and relationship dynamics
- Building found-family ensembles
- Planning character arcs across a book or series
- Creating character voice sheets for prose consistency
- Tracking relationship progression between characters

## Character Archetypes (Romantasy → Sci-Fi)

These are starting points, not cages. Subvert and combine freely.

### Female Protagonist Archetypes

| Archetype | Fantasy Origin | Sci-Fi Translation | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Huntress** | Feyre (ACOTAR) — survivor who becomes powerful | A scavenger/pilot who discovers she can fold space or bond with AI | Resourceful, adaptive, underestimated, fierce protector |
| **The Soldier** | Violet (Fourth Wing) — physically disadvantaged in a warrior's world | An undersized cadet in fleet academy who compensates with intelligence and grit | Determined, strategic, hides vulnerability behind competence |
| **The Spy** | Celaena (Throne of Glass) — deadly skills hidden behind a mask | An intelligence operative with multiple identities and a dangerous past | Guarded, competent, trust issues, devastating when the mask drops |
| **The Scholar** | Nesta (ACOSF) — rage and intellect combined | A xenolinguist or theoretical physicist whose knowledge becomes a weapon | Brilliant, abrasive, deeply wounded, power comes from understanding |

### Male Love Interest Archetypes

| Archetype | Fantasy Origin | Sci-Fi Translation | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Commander** | Rhysand (ACOTAR) — power cloaked in charm and patience | A fleet commander or wing leader who is feared by all but gentle with her | Controlled, protective without being possessive, reveals softness slowly |
| **The Rival** | Xaden (Fourth Wing) — dangerous, aligned with the enemy, irresistible | An enemy faction officer or rival cadet whose goals conflict with hers | Intense, morally gray, keeps secrets, earns trust through action not words |
| **The Shadow** | Azriel (ACOTAR) — quiet, lethal, devoted | A special operations officer or intelligence agent who works in silence | Reserved, observant, violence as precision, tender in private moments |
| **The Rogue** | Cassian (ACOTAR) — boisterous, loyal, hides depth behind humor | A fighter pilot or engineer who deflects with jokes | Charming, physically capable, emotionally perceptive, loyal to a fault |

## Romantic Pairing Design

### The Chemistry Formula

Great romantic pairings need all five elements:

1. **Friction**: They disagree, compete, or come from opposing worlds. Without friction, there's no spark.
2. **Complementarity**: Their strengths and weaknesses interlock. Together they're more than the sum.
3. **Vulnerability exchange**: Each sees a side of the other that no one else does. Mutual emotional exposure.
4. **Shared crucible**: External pressure forces them together and reveals who they truly are.
5. **Escalating stakes**: The cost of loving each other grows — faction loyalty, duty, survival, identity.

### Romantic Dynamic Patterns

| Pattern | Description | Tension Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Enemies-to-lovers** | They start opposed — rival factions, competing cadets, hunter and target | Trust must be earned through action, not words. Betrayal is always possible. |
| **Forced proximity** | Circumstances bind them — co-pilots, shared quarters, stranded together | Can't escape; must confront attraction. Physical nearness as torture. |
| **Forbidden love** | Rules, ranks, factions, or bonds prohibit them | The thrill of transgression. Every stolen moment is dangerous. |
| **Slow-burn** | Attraction builds over chapters/books before anything happens | Anticipation is the point. Each small escalation feels enormous. |
| **Fated/bonded** | A neural link, psionic bond, or genetic compatibility pairs them | Consent and choice within destiny. Do they choose the bond or fight it? |
| **Second chance** | Past lovers reunited by circumstance | History makes every interaction loaded. Old wounds meet new context. |

### Relationship Progression Tracker

Use this to track where the relationship stands at any point in the manuscript:

```markdown
## [Character A] × [Character B] — Relationship Tracker

### Phase 1: Awareness
- [ ] First meeting / first impression
- [ ] Acknowledgment of physical attraction (internal, denied)
- [ ] First conflict or disagreement

### Phase 2: Friction
- [ ] Verbal sparring / banter established
- [ ] Grudging respect earned (combat, skill, sacrifice)
- [ ] First accidental touch / physical proximity moment
- [ ] First moment of unexpected kindness

### Phase 3: Cracking
- [ ] First vulnerability shared (one-sided)
- [ ] The "almost" moment (interrupted kiss, near-confession)
- [ ] Jealousy or possessiveness surfaces
- [ ] Mutual vulnerability exchange

### Phase 4: Surrender
- [ ] First kiss or intimate moment
- [ ] Bond activation / claiming moment
- [ ] Emotional confession ("You are...")
- [ ] Physical intimacy

### Phase 5: Testing
- [ ] Secret or past revealed — trust fractures
- [ ] External pressure to separate
- [ ] Dark moment — relationship appears broken
- [ ] One character makes a sacrifice for the other

### Phase 6: Resolution
- [ ] Grand gesture / public claim
- [ ] Reunion after separation
- [ ] Fighting together as bonded pair
- [ ] New equilibrium / commitment
```

## Found Family Design

The squad/crew/wing that becomes chosen family. Essential for Yarros-style stories.

### Found Family Roles

| Role | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **The Heart** | Emotional center, keeps the group human | The one who insists on celebrations, remembers birthdays, cries first |
| **The Muscle** | Physical protector, action-oriented | Loyal, physical, may struggle with vulnerability |
| **The Brain** | Strategic thinker, problem-solver | Sees angles others miss, may overthink emotions |
| **The Wildcard** | Unpredictable, breaks tension with humor or chaos | Comic relief that hides depth; when they're serious, everyone listens |
| **The Quiet One** | Observes, supports, reveals depth slowly | Says little, but when they speak, it matters |

**Rules for Found Family**:
- Each member earns their place through shared hardship, not just proximity
- At least one member should have tension with the protagonist initially
- The group has rituals — inside jokes, traditions, shared meals, callsigns
- Betrayal by a found-family member is devastating (use sparingly)
- The group is diverse in background, personality, and skill — not a monolith

## Character Arc Design

Every major character needs an arc — a lie they believe that transforms into a truth.

| Component | Question | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **The Lie** | What false belief drives their behavior? | "I can only survive alone." |
| **The Want** | What do they pursue because of the lie? | Isolation, self-reliance, refusing help |
| **The Need** | What do they actually need (but resist)? | Connection, trust, allowing someone in |
| **The Catalyst** | What event forces them to confront the lie? | Being bonded to a partner they can't avoid |
| **The Struggle** | How do they resist the truth? | Pushing away, sabotaging the relationship, overcompensating with control |
| **The Turning Point** | What breaks the lie? | The partner saves them at great cost; they can't deny the bond |
| **The Truth** | What replaces the lie? | "Strength isn't solitude — it's choosing who to trust." |
| **The Cost** | What did growth require them to give up? | Their armor, their independence myth, their old identity |

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Perfect protagonists — flaws are features, not bugs
- ❌ Love interests who are only defined by the romance — they need their own goals, fears, and arc
- ❌ "Not like other girls" protagonists — strength doesn't require putting down other women
- ❌ Instant trust — trust is earned through action, especially in enemies-to-lovers
- ❌ Possessive behavior framed as romantic without accountability
- ❌ Supporting cast who exist only to serve the protagonist's story
- ❌ Trauma as aesthetic — if a character has a core wound, show the real impact on their behavior
- ❌ Homogeneous found family — variety in personality, background, and perspective
