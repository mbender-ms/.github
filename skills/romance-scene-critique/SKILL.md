# Romance-Scene-Critique — Intimate Scene Evaluation Skill

Evaluate romantic and intimate scenes with the expertise of a reader who has experienced hundreds of such scenes across the romantasy genre and knows exactly what makes them work.

## When to Use

- Critiquing a specific romantic tension scene (the almost-kiss, the confession, the charged moment)
- Evaluating an intimate/sexual scene for craft, emotion, and narrative purpose
- Assessing whether a scene's heat level matches the established tone and reader expectations
- Checking if steamy scenes serve the relationship arc or are gratuitous
- Reviewing consent portrayal in intimate scenes

---

## Scene Categories

### Category 1: Tension Scenes (Pre-Intimate)
The scenes that build toward physical intimacy — often more powerful than the payoff itself.

**Subtypes:**
- **The Charged Moment**: Two characters in proximity, awareness crackling. No one acts on it.
- **The Almost**: A kiss, touch, or confession that's interrupted or pulled back from at the last second.
- **Banter as Foreplay**: Verbal sparring where every line is loaded with double meaning.
- **The Care Scene**: One character tends the other's wounds, hair, distress. Intimate without being sexual.
- **Physical Training/Sparring**: Bodies in contact under the guise of combat or practice.
- **The Forced Proximity Lock-In**: Trapped together — small space, broken ship, shared quarters.

### Category 2: First Kiss / First Physical Escalation
The moment the tension breaks. One of the most important scenes in any romance.

### Category 3: Intimate Scenes (Open Door)
Full sexual/intimate scenes — explicit to varying degrees.

### Category 4: Emotional Intimacy Scenes
Not physical, but deeply intimate — confessions, vulnerability, the dropping of walls.

### Category 5: Post-Intimate Scenes
The aftermath — how characters behave after crossing a physical threshold. Often neglected but crucial.

## Evaluation Framework

### Criterion 1: Buildup — Did the Scene Earn Its Moment?

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Exquisite buildup. Chapters of tension led to this moment. When it happens, it feels inevitable AND surprising. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Strong buildup. Tension has been building. The scene feels earned. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | Adequate buildup. There's been some tension, but this could have used more runway. |
| ⭐⭐ | Rushed. The moment arrives before the reader is ready for it. |
| ⭐ | Unearned. No meaningful tension preceded this. It feels forced or random. |

**What to check:**
- How many chapters of tension preceded this scene?
- Has the reader been WANTING this to happen?
- Are there enough "almost" moments before the "finally" moment?
- Does the timing feel right for where we are in the relationship arc?

### Criterion 2: Emotional Depth — Does the Scene Reveal Character?

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Devastating. This scene cracks both characters open. We learn things about them that couldn't be revealed any other way. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Deep. Characters are vulnerable, and the intimacy reveals something important. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | Present. There's emotion, but it stays on the surface. |
| ⭐⭐ | Thin. The scene is physical but emotionally hollow. |
| ⭐ | Absent. No emotional depth whatsoever. |

**What to check:**
- Does the POV character's internal monologue reveal fears, desires, or growth?
- Does the scene show us something about the love interest we haven't seen?
- Are walls coming down? Is vulnerability present?
- After this scene, has the relationship changed in a way it can't go back from?

### Criterion 3: Pacing — Is the Scene the Right Length and Rhythm?

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Perfect pacing. Slow where it should be slow, urgent where it should be urgent. Not a wasted word. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Well-paced with minor quibbles. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | Uneven — rushes through some parts, lingers too long on others. |
| ⭐⭐ | Too fast (feels mechanical) or too slow (loses momentum). |
| ⭐ | Severely mis-paced. Either a sprint through what should be savored or an interminable slog. |

**What to check:**
- Does the scene breathe? Is there space for the reader to feel?
- Are physical details balanced with emotional reaction?
- Do sentence lengths vary to match the energy? (Short for urgency, long for savoring)
- Is the scene the right LENGTH for its importance? (A first kiss should be longer than a routine kiss)

### Criterion 4: Heat Calibration — Is the Explicitness Right for This Book?

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Perfectly calibrated. The heat level matches the book's tone and the scene's emotional weight. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Mostly right — one or two moments feel slightly over or under for the tone. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | Noticeable mismatch — the heat level feels jarring in context. |
| ⭐⭐ | Significantly miscalibrated — too explicit or too restrained for what the story has been building to. |
| ⭐ | Completely wrong — either shockingly explicit in a mild book or frustratingly restrained after intense buildup. |

**What to check:**
- Does the heat level match what the rest of the book has established?
- If heat escalates, is the escalation gradual and earned?
- Is the explicit content CHARACTER-DRIVEN (we see these specific people, not generic bodies)?
- Does the scene use the sci-fi setting in the intimate moment? (Neural-link feedback, zero-g, bond resonance)

### Criterion 5: Consent and Agency

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Crystal clear. Both characters have agency. Desire is mutual, expressed, and respected. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Clear. Consent is present, perhaps more implicit than explicit, but unmistakable. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | Adequate but could be clearer. No red flags, but the reader might pause. |
| ⭐⭐ | Ambiguous. Power dynamics or unclear communication create discomfort. |
| ⭐ | Problematic. Consent is absent, coerced, or glossed over. Must be addressed. |

**What to check:**
- Do both characters actively choose this? Is there verbal or clear non-verbal consent?
- Are power dynamics (rank, abilities, bonds) acknowledged?
- If a bond/link creates desire, does the narrative interrogate that? Is there still choice?
- Can either character say no? Would no be respected?
- Are intoxicants, emotional distress, or power imbalances present? If so, are they handled thoughtfully?

### Criterion 6: Narrative Purpose — Does This Scene NEED to Exist?

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Essential. Remove this scene and the relationship arc breaks. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Important. The scene advances the relationship significantly. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | Useful. Adds to the relationship but isn't strictly necessary. |
| ⭐⭐ | Redundant. Previous scenes already accomplished what this scene does. |
| ⭐ | Gratuitous. Exists purely for heat, not story. Could be cut without loss. |

**What to check:**
- What does the reader learn from this scene that they didn't know before?
- Does the relationship change because of this scene?
- If you cut this scene, would the arc still make sense? If yes, consider whether it's needed.
- Does this scene do something NO OTHER scene in the book does?

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Being squeamish — evaluate intimate scenes with the same analytical rigor as action scenes
- ❌ Judging heat level morally — assess whether it serves the story, not whether you personally prefer it
- ❌ Treating intimate scenes as separate from the story — they ARE the story; they must advance character and plot
- ❌ Ignoring consent dynamics because "it's fiction" — readers notice, and problematic consent pulls readers out
- ❌ Demanding more explicit content when the tone doesn't call for it — or demanding less when it's earned
- ❌ Forgetting the sci-fi setting during critique — the best intimate scenes in this genre USE the speculative elements
