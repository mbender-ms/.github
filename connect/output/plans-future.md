## Plan for the future

### What are your goals for the upcoming period?

#### Goal 1: Deliver secure, high-quality content for customers

1. [Rank 1] By October 2026, complete the TSG migration for the remaining top 12 services (DNS, Private Link, Firewall, DDoS Protection, Web Application Firewall, Load Balancer, Virtual WAN). Improving discoverability of troubleshooting content reduces support volume and helps customers self-serve. Measured by TSG migration completion %, content retrievability scores, and page views on migrated articles.

2. [Rank 2] By October 2026, optimize content for copilot retrievability via Knowledge service integration across App Gateway, Bastion, and VPN Gateway. Customers increasingly rely on copilot-powered queries to find answers; content that isn't retrievable is invisible. Measured by retrievability rate improvement and copilot-surfaced content metrics.

3. [Rank 2.5] By June 30, 2026, ship the Azure Networking Plan & Design Guide — all 18 articles (1 hub + 17 capability modules) live on Learn with PM sign-off through all five phases. Co-authored with Duong Au. Closes the gap where customers lack scenario-based, design-led guidance and rely on disconnected service-centric how-tos. Phased delivery: Phase 0 Apr 10, Phase 1 Apr 30, Phase 2 May 23, Phase 3 Jun 6, Phase 4 Jun 25, Phase 5 Jun 30. Measured by articles published, PM confirmations per phase, and feedback from architects and network admins.

4. [Rank 3] By October 2026, address the top 3 CSS issues for the top 5 services in collaboration with TAs and CSS. Reducing support instances by resolving common problems through documentation. Measured by CSS issue resolution rate and reduction in repeat support tickets for targeted issues.

5. [Rank 4] By October 2026, fact-check content across service repositories (Application Gateway, Load Balancer, AVNM, Front Door, Web Application Firewall) for stale or inconsistent information. Deprecation dates, feature availability, and cross-service references drift over time — when updates occur, articles get missed. Collaboration with Rayo's and Akash's teams. Baseline: freshness skew >365 days; UUF complaints citing outdated info. Measured by defect-free page views, fresh page views, and reduction in articles >365 days stale.

6. [Rank 5] By October 2026, remediate duplicate or contradictory content in Virtual Network, VPN Gateway, Load Balancer, Web Application Firewall, and Front Door. Collaboration with Allen Sudbring, Rayo's team, and Akash. Baseline: Virtual Network — 3 contradictions + 4 dupes; VPN Gateway — 2 contradictions + 6 dupes; Load Balancer — 3 contradictions + 4 dupes. Measured by high-priority items remediated from contradiction/duplication reports.

7. [Rank 7] By October 2026, identify content gaps and issues through customer sources beyond our current datasets — Microsoft Q&A, MVP distribution lists, Teams channels, and CXP input. No systematic gap inventory exists today; sourcing is ad-hoc. Measured by count of externally sourced gaps identified and fixed, reduction in gap-related negative verbatims, and improved defect-free page views post-fix.

#### Goal 2: Continuously improve and innovate

1. [Rank 6] By September 2026, enhance AI skills across the team through structured knowledge-sharing sessions (brown bags). Run at least three sessions covering AI-assisted workflows, copilot tools for retrievability, and content quality automation. Sharing ideas and tools helps the whole team deliver better products for customers. Measured by reuse of shared workflows, peer feedback, and mentions of AI tools in PRs.

2. [Rank 6.5] By September 2026, research and integrate Azure Network Zero Trust Assessment findings into our documentation. Customers struggle to find security recommendations because most docs weren't written with Zero Trust and security-first principles. Assessments provide a baseline of recommended best practices we can use to improve content, promote assessments, and improve visibility of secure network practices. Measured by organic search rate increase for security pages and click-through rate into guidance.

3. [Rank 8] By November 2026, use AI to improve readability and content quality across Application Gateway and Load Balancer documents. Of 723 total articles across both services, 269 have a bounce rate over 20% — indicating potential quality or readability issues. Measured by bounce rate reduction on targeted articles and copy-try-scroll rate improvement.

4. [Rank 5.5] By October 2026, build an integrated TOC for Azure Networking and Network Fundamentals. The two areas currently have disjointed TOCs with excessive cross-linking and duplicated content, creating confusion and making it hard for customers to navigate and find the right content. Measured by TOC consolidation completion, reduction in duplicated navigation paths, and customer navigation feedback.

5. By November 2026, ship one repeatable workflow for common maintenance updates that cuts end-to-end cycle time by 15% versus today. Apply AI-assisted quality checks to 100% of priority troubleshooting updates, reducing first-pass revisions by 25%. Measured by workflow adoption rate, revision queue depth, and time to publish.

Measured by phase completion dates met, PM sign-offs received, and documented next steps tracked after each review.

4. By October 2026, run three cross-service collaboration sessions with PMs, engineers, and writers to align on scenario design priorities, CSS resolution strategy, and quality standards. At least five improvements implemented based on attendee input. Measured by session attendance, documented decisions, and tracked implementations.
